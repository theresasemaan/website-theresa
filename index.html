Imaginary Creatures & Medieval Thought

In medieval Europe, imaginary creatures were not fantasies — they were symbols, warnings, and lessons encoded in visual form.

Bestiaries combined theology, folklore, and observation. Creatures at the edges of the known world embodied fear of the unknown, moral conflict, and humanity’s attempt to explain what could not be proven.

This Archive revisits that tradition. Each creature exists not as proof, but as a question — preserved as ink, interpretation, and memory.

How the Archive Is Read

The Archive is not meant to be consumed quickly.

Each image, description, and fragment is presented without final explanation. As in medieval manuscripts, meaning is layered, partial, and sometimes contradictory.

Visitors are encouraged to read slowly, observe margins, and accept uncertainty as part of the record.

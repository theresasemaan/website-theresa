The Nature of the Auction

These auctions do not trade objects alone — they trade custodianship.

Each lot presented in the Archive is derived from a fragment, illustration, or textual reference whose origin is incomplete, disputed, or symbolic. The act of bidding is not ownership, but agreement to preserve the narrative attached to the piece.

Successful bidders receive documentation, reproduction rights, and a formal record of custodianship within the Archive. No claim of historical authenticity is implied.

Conditions of Participation

Bidding is ceremonial and time-bound.

All auctions close at predetermined hours. Once concluded, a lot is withdrawn and may never reappear in the same form.

The Archive reserves the right to withhold fragments whose symbolic weight exceeds scholarly clarity.
